Rainwater Harvesting

Rainwater harvesting is the process of collecting and storing rainwater
for later use. Instead of letting the rainwater flow away, we collect
it from rooftops, land surfaces, or rock catchments and store it in tanks or underground.

Facts Information

  • Rainwater is generally clean but should be filtered before use.
  • It helps recharge underground water reserves.
  • This system is highly beneficial in areas facing water shortages.
  • Improper storage can lead to water contamination.
Reduces Water Bills

Collecting rainwater reduces dependency on municipal supply, thus lowering water bills significantly over time.

Residential Rainwater Filter

Residential filters purify rainwater before storage, removing dirt, debris, and contaminants for safe use.

Rainwater harvesting systems are affordable to install and require minimal maintenance, making them ideal for households.

Methods Information

    Rooftop Rainwater Harvesting
  • Rooftop harvesting (collecting water from roofs).
  • Rainwater is collected from rooftops through pipes and stored in tanks or underground reservoirs.
  • Filters are used to remove leaves, dirt, and debris.
    • Surface Runoff Harvesting
  • Surface storage of rainwater.
  • Rainwater flowing from roads, parks, and open spaces is collected in tanks or reservoirs.
  • Suitable for large public spaces and institutions.
    • Groundwater Recharge System
  • Groundwater recharge through rainwater.
  • Rainwater is directed into recharge structures such as recharge wells, soak pits, or percolation trenches.
  • Helps raise the groundwater table, especially in urban areas.
    • Check Dams, Ponds, and Lakes
  • Construction of check dams and ponds to retain water.
  • Small barriers or ponds are built in rural areas to collect rainwater.
  • Stored water slowly seeps into the ground, improving water availability for nearby regions.
Surface Rainwater Harvesting

Surface rainwater harvesting captures rainwater that has fallen on the ground in open fields or paved areas. The water is then collected in tanks or reservoirs for storage and future use.

Rooftop Rainwater Harvesting

Rooftop rainwater harvesting collects rainwater from roofs and stores it in tanks or underground reservoirs. The collected water is filtered before being stored for future use.

Benefits

  • Conserves water resources.
  • Reduce flood risks.
  • Increases availability of clean drinking water.
  • Maintains environmental balance.
  • Improves agricultural productivity.
  • Aids in groundwater recharge.
  • Cost-effective and eco-friendly solution to water crisis.

Rainwater Harvesting

Rainwater harvesting is a smart way to conserve water by collecting and storing rain from rooftops, surfaces, or natural catchments. This stored rainwater can be used for gardening, household cleaning, irrigation, or even filtered for drinking purposes. It's an eco-friendly method that helps reduce dependency on municipal water, cuts water bills, and supports sustainable living. Let's save water—one drop at a time!
