Commercial Rainwater Harvesting

Effective solutions for offices, schools, hospitals, and industrial units to save water and reduce costs.

Techniques for Commercial Use

Percolation Tanks

A percolation tank is a man-made structure designed to capture and store rainwater, allowing it to percolate into the ground and recharge groundwater levels. These tanks are often used in arid regions to enhance water availability for agriculture and drinking, improving water conservation and sustainability..
Check Dams

Check dams are small, temporary structures built across streams or rivers to slow water flow, reduce soil erosion, and promote water infiltration. They help in replenishing groundwater, improving irrigation, and preventing floods. Check dams are particularly effective in arid and semi-arid regions for sustainable water management and conservation.

Recommended Products

Large Storage Tanks

Large storage tanks are essential infrastructure used to store vast quantities of liquids or gases, such as water, oil, or chemicals. They are commonly used in industries, agriculture, and municipal systems. These tanks ensure a reliable supply, efficient distribution, and maintain safety standards in managing bulk storage requirements.

Industrial Filters

Industrial filters are devices used to remove impurities, particles, and contaminants from liquids, gases, and air in various industrial processes. They play a crucial role in ensuring product quality, protecting equipment, and maintaining environmental standards. Common applications include water treatment, HVAC systems, and manufacturing processes, where precise filtration is necessary to enhance efficiency, safety, and sustainability.

Tips for Institutions & Industries

  • Plan rainwater collection during building design phase.
  • Monitor and maintain underground recharge systems.
  • Use automated level sensors to manage water levels.
Rainwater Harvesting

Rainwater harvesting is a smart way to conserve water by collecting and storing rain from rooftops, surfaces, or natural catchments. This stored rainwater can be used for gardening, household cleaning, irrigation, or even filtered for drinking purposes. It's an eco-friendly method that helps reduce dependency on municipal water, cuts water bills, and supports sustainable living. Let's save water—one drop at a time!
